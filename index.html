Home Purchase Incentive Program

What is it and why should I care?

Are you a realtor? Real Estate Broker? A Homeowner looking to move?

Our guess is that if you fall into any of these categories, that you want to sell a home. This program is strategically designed to incentivize a prospective home buyer into making a purchase and works in synergy with our other marketing products to increase the probability of a home selling. The program is free—it literally costs you nothing. The only caveat is that in order to participate you must have bought at least one of our photo or video products. This is a very good deal when you consider that our photo and video products have some of the most competitive pricing on the market. The cost of the program is not baked into those products.

The program entices prospective home buyers into making a purchase by of - fering them the opportunity to get funding for various money-saving sustain - ability projects, like roof-top solar panels , and energy efficiency in addi - tion to funding for permaculture style carbon gardens . These gardens are aesthetically pleasing landscaping in their own right, and can include food producing plants at the prospective-buyers request.

The idea of getting roof-top solar panels, energy efficiency upgrades, and car - bon gardens is exciting in and of itself because of the environmental aspect. In addition to this, the projects are specifically designed to lower the month - ly overhead of someone who purchases and moves into the home. This can help ease some of the anxiety a prospector buyer can feel when they look at a home and realize their overheard would be higher than their current living situation. There are no fees or costs.

Priority Funding for the prospective buyer. Sounds great, but what does this mean?

Priority funding status means that if the prospective buyer makes a purchase, funders on ’s ™ platform will be directed to this particular home’s proj - ects over other projects which are also looking for funding on the site. In this sense, they have priority over other homes which may be on the platform.

When a prospective buyer expresses interest in purchasing the home, you can let them know that it is enrolled in the home purchase incentive program and that they may qualify for funding if they purchase the home. You are allowed to market your enrollment in the program—however, we ask that you explain that it is pos - sible that the home may not qualify for funding if it does not meet ’s ™ eligibility requirements and that the funding campaign could fail to reach 100% of the quote. This is the case with any Crowdfunding platform. If funding fails to reach 100% of the project quote, the prospective buyer.

Why would a 3rd party fund these projects? What are Carbon Rights?

Technically, no one is directly funding the project. Funders on ™ pur - chase the “Carbon Rights” to projects that reduce greenhouse gas emissions. This is why we can only offer enrollment in the program after a prospective buyer makes a home purchase. Once they own the home, they are able to sell the ‘carbon rights’ to funders on the platform, who in return provide the funds needed to implement the project. Ownership of Carbon Rights allows you to claim responsibility for the greenhouse-gas emission reduction caused by the sustainability project. In other words, if someone owns the carbon rights to the solar panels on your roof, they are the only ones who can claim that they have done something ‘green’ in relation to these projects. The prospective buyer receives all of the financial benefits of reduced overhead from the solar panels and efficiency upgrade, but cannot claim they are re - sponsible for reducing carbon emissions—this is owned by the project funders, based on the % of the quote that was committed.

Although we hope this does not occur, we cannot control the actions of funders on the platform so there is al - ways the possibility that funding will not reach 100% of the project costs, or receive any funding at all.

It’s important that you understand and consider these limitations, but also keep in mind that we do not charge for enrollment into the program, so there is very little risk.
